## Section 4 — INSIGHTS NOT IN PRIOR RESEARCH

### A. Mental Health / Burnout Journey (Specifics)

**Tokyo aftermath (2021):** Relationship to swimming "in ruins"; hated the sport; took full year off.  
**Year off activities:** Deliberately wanted to lift weights; used strength training as mental reset.  
**Closure framework:** "Closure either way"—meaning whether new method works or fails, he had psychological peace.  
**Post-Paris mentality:** Got engaged, had first son; intentionally deprioritized sponsorship/media to protect family life.  
**Implication:** Mental health isn't separate from training; it's foundational. His willingness to walk away made radical training shift possible.

### B. Why Squat Was Retired (Ankle Mobility Context)

Cam's calves are "horrific" (his word). Ankle stiffness prevents full squat depth without heels rising; limiting risk at heavy loads. Rather than force it with mobility work, he switched to **trap bar deadlift from the ground** (which accommodates his ankle constraint). Shows pragmatic adaptation: train around limitations rather than fight them.

### C. Phelps/Hackett at Miami Swim Club (Vivid Childhood Memory)

Training at Miami Swimming Club on Gold Coast, he watched Michael Phelps and Grant Hackett training alongside him as a "grom" (young kid). This wasn't aspirational poster-watching; it was **seeing their bodies move in the water in real-time**, then mentally simulating those movements for his own sessions. Visual-kinesthetic bridge.

### D. Track Sprinters as Benchmark (200m Track ≈ 50m Pool)

Cam explicitly draws parallels between track sprinting (200m run) and 50m freestyle. Both are peak-power events; both require force production over short distance. This analogy guides his gym philosophy: **pulling strength, not aerobic capacity**.

### E. "Extremely Rectangular" Body Framing

Cam is 185 cm tall but has a 203 cm armspan—18 cm advantage. He calls this "extremely rectangular" (long limbs, shorter torso). This unusual ratio may explain:
- Why high pull-ups suit him  
- Why his kick is primarily for balance/timing (shorter, powerful legs less effective as primary propulsion)  
- Why his 5–10m segment is "significantly slower" than top-end sprinters (body shape disadvantage in first 5m)

### F. Bench Press Finally "Works" (Recent Conversion)

Bench press was historically absent from his training. Only recently added (dumbbell phase, then barbell testing). No explicit reason given for avoidance, but context suggests: **swim world historically neglected horizontal-pushing strength**. His adoption signals recognition of missing element.

### G. Junior Swimmer Development Under Tim Lane / Matt Smith

Cam actively mentors younger swimmers (Akira, Katu, others mentioned) using his training principles. This isn't theoretical; he's testing on next generation. The podcast reveals junior coaches (e.g., speaker in timestamp 153–158) crediting Cam + Tim as foundational mentors. **Suggests a movement, not just an individual outlier.**

### H. "Closure Either Way" Decision Philosophy

After Tokyo, Cam was at a mental breaking point. His decision to abandon traditional training was emotionally framed as: "I had closure either way"—meaning *success or failure, I'm OK*. This psychological frame freed him to experiment radically without fear of "wasting" a cycle. Paradoxically, reduced pressure → better results.

### I. Weight Gain During Strength Blocks (87 kg Paris → 94 kg April)

Post-Paris, he did a 5-month strength-focused block (Oct '24–March '25) with minimal swimming. Gained 7 kg (87→94). This is treated as **expected, not problematic**. Suggests disciplined periodization: different phases allow different body compositions.

### J. Heel Rise Issue in Squat / Remediation on Slant Board

Calves too tight for full squat. Uses slant board to challenge dorsiflexion. Still couldn't achieve safe heavy squat, so abandoned it. Shows data-driven decision-making: *if the exercise becomes risky or limiting, replace it rather than forcing it.*

### K. Upper-Body Technique: "Hollow Body" Activation

Core technical innovation: Hollow body hold (gymnastics-derived) in water, then slowly extending through streamline while maintaining that core tension. Different from traditional "reach and relax"; aims for **isometric trunk rigidity** while limbs oscillate around it.

### L. Hollow Body in Squat Position (Mobility Work)

Uses full squat position to challenge hip flexion mobility; combined with hollow body activation teaches: *I can arch + extend lats even when hips are flexed*. Directly transfers to freestyle body position.

### M. Kick's Primary Role (Balance, Not Propulsion)

In the 50m, Cam believes kick is primarily for **balance, timing, linking upper-body force generation**—not forward propulsion. Underwater footage shows him flat until hips, then hips drop, knees extend. Kick manages that hip-knee sequencing, not drives the body forward.
