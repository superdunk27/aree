## Cross-cutting principles (Aree's decision rules)

These should guide ALL future Toey-learns-X conversations:

### From doc 01 (motor learning fundamentals)
- **Block early, randomize later** — early skill acquisition benefits from blocked practice (technique focus). Past basic competence, switch to random/interleaved (better retention even though it feels worse)
- **External focus beats internal** (d=0.5-0.8 across many tasks) — cue the *outcome* in the environment, not body parts. "Push the floor away" beats "extend your hips"
- **Faded feedback** — give frequent feedback early, taper to less frequent as competence develops. Learner will protest. Don't capitulate
- **Desirable difficulties** — spacing, interleaving, retrieval practice all FEEL inefficient and ARE efficient. Performance during practice ≠ retention
- **The 10,000 hours rule is folk-wisdom** — Macnamara meta shows practice explains 18-26% of skill variance, not 80%+
- **Mental practice works** (d≈0.4) but only after technical model exists — pure imagery without correct motor template just rehearses errors
- **Constraints-led approach** (Newell) — manipulate the environment to elicit the right pattern, often beats verbal instruction

### From doc 02 (adult learner)
- **Plasticity declines gradually, not abruptly** — there's no "critical period" cliff for motor skills. Language has the closest thing to a critical period, and even that's permeable
- **Adult advantages**: metacognition, focused practice, life context, identity stability — adults often *out-learn* kids per practice hour when motivated
- **Procedural >> declarative for age preservation** — the system that learns motor skills holds up. The system that learns facts/words decays first
- **BDNF priming via exercise** — light cardio 20-40 min before skill practice may potentiate consolidation (animal-strong, human-suggestive)
- **Sleep matters MORE for adults** — consolidation efficiency drops; protect sleep around new-skill phases
- **Identity beats hours** — "I'm a person who's learning swim mechanics" trajectory > "I need to grind 10,000 hours" trajectory (Dweck mindset, expectancy effects)

## Honest gaps

- **Effect sizes from secondary sources** — agent flagged that some Wulf/Schmidt effect-size numbers came from review summaries not the canonical Schmidt textbook 6th ed. Round 5 candidate: lock in primary-source numbers
- **Bondarchuk block periodization** for skill mesocycles — cut for word budget; relevant for any structured skill program
- **Adult-onset longitudinal data** — most "adult takes X months to Y" claims are community reports, not peer-reviewed cohorts. Specific to gymnastics, music, language
- **Hensch's molecular brake / critical period reopening** — translation from animal models to human skill learning is mostly unverified

## How Aree should use this hub

When Toey is starting/struggling/plateauing on ANY skill:
1. Identify stage (Fitts-Posner: cognitive → associative → autonomous)
2. Match practice variability to stage (block→random)
3. Match feedback frequency to stage (high→faded)
4. Apply external focus cues
5. Add desirable difficulties (spacing, interleaving)
6. Match expected timeline to age (and don't undersell adult advantages)

For physical skills specifically: combine with Round 2 (`ψ/learn/strength-training/`) and Round 3 (`ψ/learn/cross-domain/`) — *what to train* + *how to learn it*.
