## Cross-cutting synthesis

### What track cycling, kayak sprint, free diving, and hand-balancing all teach the swim coach

1. **Joint-angle specificity beats movement specificity** — Lum's 120° elbow isometric (#02) and gymnastics' end-range loaded mobility (#04) and track cycling's standing-start force application (#01) all point to: *train the angle/position you'll race in*, not just the movement pattern. Generic "compound lift" thinking misses this.

2. **Strength-to-mass has a plateau, then velocity wins** — track cycling (#01) shows the current GOAT (Lavreysen) wins on mechanical efficiency over a 2.6× BW squatter (Förstemann). McEvoy's bodyweight increase (87→94 kg) was about *force capacity in water*, not raw lifting numbers. Past 1.75-2× BW for swim, additional load returns diminish.

3. **The "fluid environment" is its own training space** — kayak (#02) and swim are the only two ATP-PCr sprint events against fluid. Tethered/leashed protocols, force-application-against-water, and isometric core-against-stroke-asymmetry are concepts that exist in kayak literature but rarely in swim. The bidirectional flow is open.

4. **Breath economy is a trainable skill, not a fixed parameter** — free diving (#03) makes clear that what limits sprint swim breath-holding is CO2 tolerance + dive reflex underdevelopment, NOT lung capacity. The skill is built dry; transfers to wet.

5. **Mobility is durability investment, not performance magic** — hand-balancing (#04) honest critique of the McEvoy mobility narrative. The masters sprinter (Toey is 31) gets the biggest ROI from mobility-as-injury-prevention + scapular control, not from chasing the dive-time drop story.

### Where the docs disagree (or expose tensions)

- **Strength benchmarks** — Track cycling's "2.5× BW squat" target vs swim's "size doesn't swim, force does" → both can be right; the cycling number is for a 10-13s pure power event with no upper-body specificity, the swim philosophy is for a 22s event with significant upper-body and aerobic contribution. Different events, different optima.
- **Breath training risk-vs-reward** — free diving offers protocols, but the safety section is non-trivial. Hypoxic training in pool has caused fatalities. Adoption requires informed risk acceptance.
- **Mobility evidence quality** — gymnastics-derived loaded mobility has effect-size literature, but the swim-specific transfer is mostly mechanistic + n=1 (McEvoy).

## Honest gaps (Round 4 candidates if Toey wants more)

Aggregated from agent reports:

1. **Track cycling has zero upper-body analog to swim catch** — must look elsewhere (gymnastics, throws, Vasa swim trainer, in-water)
2. **No swim-specific RCT on isometric strength inclusion → 50m race time** — Lum & Barbosa is kayak; transfer is by analogy
3. **No swim-specific RCT on dry CO2 tolerance tables alone** — Robertson 2021 used dynamic apnea (in water)
4. **Wrist injury epidemiology in adult hand-balancers** — Marmerstein flags clinically, no published rates
5. **English-language access to Hungarian/German kayak program internals** — practitioner sources only
6. **EscapeCollective Dutch sprint cycling deep-dive** — paywalled; Hoogland/Lavreysen primary methodology incomplete
